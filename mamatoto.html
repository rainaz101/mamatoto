🏥 What is Mamatoto Village?

Mamatoto Village is a nonprofit maternal health organization that provides comprehensive support to mothers and families throughout their pregnancy, childbirth, and postpartum journey. The name "Mamatoto" combines "Mama" (mother) and "Toto" (child), representing our commitment to supporting both mother and child as a unit.

Founded with the vision of reducing maternal health disparities and ensuring every family has access to quality care, we serve as a community hub where mothers can find education, support, and compassionate care from trained professionals and experienced volunteers.

🎯 Our Mission & Vision

Mission: To provide comprehensive maternal health services, education, and support to ensure every mother and family has access to quality care during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period. We believe in the power of community, education, and compassionate care to transform maternal health outcomes.

Vision: A world where every mother receives the care, support, and education she needs to have a healthy pregnancy, safe childbirth, and confident parenting journey. We envision communities where maternal health disparities are eliminated and every family thrives.

🧠 Understanding Mental Health Scores

What is the Mental Health Score?

The Mental Health Score is a simple 1-10 scale used to assess a client's emotional well-being and mental health status during their pregnancy and postpartum journey. This score helps our team provide appropriate support and track improvements over time.

Score Guidelines:
  • 1-3 (Low): Client may be experiencing significant stress, anxiety, or depression. Requires immediate attention and support.
  • 4-6 (Moderate): Client shows some signs of stress or emotional challenges. Benefits from regular check-ins and support services.
  • 7-8 (Good): Client is generally coping well with some normal pregnancy/postpartum adjustments.
  • 9-10 (Excellent): Client is thriving emotionally and has strong support systems in place.

Note: This score is assessed through conversations with clients and is used to ensure they receive appropriate mental health support. Higher scores indicate better emotional well-being and coping abilities.

Project Brief: Mamatoto Village Dashboard

Executive Summary

The Mamatoto Village Dashboard is a comprehensive web-based management system designed to streamline operations, track key performance indicators, and enhance the delivery of maternal health services. This project addresses the critical need for efficient data management and real-time monitoring in nonprofit maternal health organizations.

Project Objectives
  • Centralize client and volunteer management
  • Track financial performance and donor relationships
  • Monitor service delivery and outcomes
  • Provide real-time analytics and reporting
  • Improve operational efficiency and decision-making
Key Features
  • Real-time dashboard with KPIs
  • Client services tracking and management
  • Volunteer and doula assignment system
  • Donation and expense tracking
  • Task management and workflow automation
  • Comprehensive reporting and analytics
Expected Outcomes

The implementation of this dashboard is expected to result in a 25% improvement in operational efficiency, 30% reduction in administrative overhead, and enhanced ability to demonstrate impact to stakeholders and donors. The system will support Mamatoto Village's mission to provide quality maternal health services to underserved communities.

Client Experience Journey Map

📢

1. Awareness & Discovery

Touchpoints: Social media, referrals, healthcare providers, community events

Client Actions: Research services, read testimonials, check eligibility

Pain Points: Information overload, uncertainty about services

Success Metrics: Website visits, social media engagement, referral rates

📞

2. Initial Contact & Intake

Touchpoints: Phone calls, online forms, in-person visits

Client Actions: Complete intake forms, provide medical history, discuss needs

Pain Points: Long wait times, complex paperwork, privacy concerns

Success Metrics: Response time, completion rates, client satisfaction scores

🔍

3. Assessment & Planning

Touchpoints: Initial consultations, health assessments, goal setting

Client Actions: Meet with doula, discuss birth plan, set health goals

Pain Points: Anxiety about pregnancy, uncertainty about options

Success Metrics: Mental health scores, goal completion rates, client confidence levels

🤱

4. Prenatal Care & Support

Touchpoints: Regular checkups, education sessions, support groups

Client Actions: Attend appointments, participate in classes, build support network

Pain Points: Physical discomfort, scheduling conflicts, information gaps

Success Metrics: Attendance rates, health outcomes, knowledge retention

👶

5. Birth & Delivery Support

Touchpoints: Labor support, hospital coordination, birth plan execution

Client Actions: Receive doula support, communicate with medical team

Pain Points: Labor pain, medical interventions, unexpected complications

Success Metrics: Birth satisfaction, intervention rates, safety outcomes

🏠

6. Postpartum Care & Recovery

Touchpoints: Home visits, breastfeeding support, mental health check-ins

Client Actions: Receive home care, attend follow-up appointments

Pain Points: Sleep deprivation, breastfeeding challenges, postpartum depression

Success Metrics: Recovery rates, breastfeeding success, mental health improvement

🎓

7. Graduation & Community Integration

Touchpoints: Final assessments, community referrals, alumni programs

Client Actions: Complete program, provide feedback, join alumni network

Pain Points: Transition anxiety, loss of support network

Success Metrics: Program completion rates, long-term outcomes, community engagement
