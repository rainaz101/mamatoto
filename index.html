What is Mamatoto Village?

Mamatoto Village is devoted to serving Black women through the creation of career pathways in maternal health and providing accessible perinatal support services designed to equip Black women with the necessary tools to make the most informed decisions in their maternity care, parenting, and lives. The name "Mamatoto" combines "Mama" (mother) and "Toto" (child), representing our commitment to supporting both mother and child as a unit.

Founded with the vision of combating racism and health disparities for Black mothers, babies, and their families, we serve as a community hub where Black women can find education, support, and compassionate care from trained professionals and experienced volunteers.

Our Mission & Vision

Mission: Mamatoto Village is devoted to serving Black women through the creation of career pathways in maternal health and providing accessible perinatal support services designed to equip Black women with the necessary tools to make the most informed decisions in their maternity care, parenting, and lives.

Vision: Healthy Mamas. Healthy Babies. Healthy Communities. We envision a world where Black women have access to the information, resources, autonomy, and safety they need to preserve their lives, the lives of their children, and long-term interests. We believe that Black women can be strengthened by one another, giving rise to a more radical, collective, and supportive environment for wellness and thriving.

Community Impact

Our community-driven approach has transformed the lives of countless Black families. Through dedicated volunteers, compassionate care providers, and strong community partnerships, we've created a network of support that extends far beyond individual services. We foster a space for resistance, restoration, and reclamation fueled by Black women's collective dreams of a more just world to live, love, birth, raise children, and sustain our families.

Every day, we witness the power of community in action - from the doulas who provide round-the-clock support to the Black families who find strength in knowing they're not alone on their journey. Together, we're building a healthier, more supportive world for Black mothers, babies, and families.

Kinship & Support

At the heart of Mamatoto Village is the belief that kinship and community support are essential for Black maternal health. We create spaces where Black women can connect, share experiences, and build lasting relationships that sustain them through their journey. We believe that Black women can be strengthened by one another, giving rise to a more radical, collective, and supportive environment for wellness and thriving.

Our kinship programs bring together Black women from diverse backgrounds, creating a network of support that extends beyond our services. These connections often become lifelong friendships, providing emotional support and practical assistance when it's needed most. We center and amplify the voices of the Black women and families we serve.

🧠 Understanding Mental Health Scores

What is the Mental Health Score?

The Mental Health Score is a simple 1-10 scale used to assess a client's emotional well-being and mental health status during their pregnancy and postpartum journey. This score helps our team provide appropriate support and track improvements over time.

Score Guidelines:
  • 1-3 (Low): Client may be experiencing significant stress, anxiety, or depression. Requires immediate attention and support.
  • 4-6 (Moderate): Client shows some signs of stress or emotional challenges. Benefits from regular check-ins and support services.
  • 7-8 (Good): Client is generally coping well with some normal pregnancy/postpartum adjustments.
  • 9-10 (Excellent): Client is thriving emotionally and has strong support systems in place.

Note: This score is assessed through conversations with clients and is used to ensure they receive appropriate mental health support. Higher scores indicate better emotional well-being and coping abilities.

Project Brief: Mamatoto Village Maternal Health Hub

Executive Summary

The Mamatoto Village Maternal Health Hub is a comprehensive web-based management system designed to streamline operations, track key performance indicators, and enhance the delivery of maternal health services to Black women and families. This project addresses the critical need for efficient data management and real-time monitoring in nonprofit maternal health organizations serving Black communities.

Project Objectives
  • Centralize client and volunteer management
  • Track financial performance and donor relationships
  • Monitor service delivery and outcomes
  • Provide real-time analytics and reporting
  • Improve operational efficiency and decision-making
Key Features
  • Real-time hub with KPIs
  • Client services tracking and management
  • Volunteer and doula assignment system
  • Donation and expense tracking
  • Task management and workflow automation
  • Comprehensive reporting and analytics
Expected Outcomes

The implementation of this hub is expected to result in a 25% improvement in operational efficiency, 30% reduction in administrative overhead, and enhanced ability to demonstrate impact to stakeholders and donors. The system will support Mamatoto Village's mission to provide quality maternal health services to Black women and families, combating racism and health disparities in Black communities.

Client Experience Journey Map

📢

1. Awareness & Discovery

Touchpoints: Social media, referrals, healthcare providers, community events

Client Actions: Research services, read testimonials, check eligibility

Pain Points: Information overload, uncertainty about services

Success Metrics: Website visits, social media engagement, referral rates

📞

2. Initial Contact & Intake

Touchpoints: Phone calls, online forms, in-person visits

Client Actions: Complete intake forms, provide medical history, discuss needs

Pain Points: Long wait times, complex paperwork, privacy concerns

Success Metrics: Response time, completion rates, client satisfaction scores

🔍

3. Assessment & Planning

Touchpoints: Initial consultations, health assessments, goal setting

Client Actions: Meet with doula, discuss birth plan, set health goals

Pain Points: Anxiety about pregnancy, uncertainty about options

Success Metrics: Mental health scores, goal completion rates, client confidence levels

🤱

4. Prenatal Care & Support

Touchpoints: Regular checkups, education sessions, support groups

Client Actions: Attend appointments, participate in classes, build support network

Pain Points: Physical discomfort, scheduling conflicts, information gaps

Success Metrics: Attendance rates, health outcomes, knowledge retention

👶

5. Birth & Delivery Support

Touchpoints: Labor support, hospital coordination, birth plan execution

Client Actions: Receive doula support, communicate with medical team

Pain Points: Labor pain, medical interventions, unexpected complications

Success Metrics: Birth satisfaction, intervention rates, safety outcomes

🏠

6. Postpartum Care & Recovery

Touchpoints: Home visits, breastfeeding support, mental health check-ins

Client Actions: Receive home care, attend follow-up appointments

Pain Points: Sleep deprivation, breastfeeding challenges, postpartum depression

Success Metrics: Recovery rates, breastfeeding success, mental health improvement

🎓

7. Graduation & Community Integration

Touchpoints: Final assessments, community referrals, alumni programs

Client Actions: Complete program, provide feedback, join alumni network

Pain Points: Transition anxiety, loss of support network

Success Metrics: Program completion rates, long-term outcomes, community engagement
